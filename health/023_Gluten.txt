Gluten (from Latin :wikt:gluten#Latin|gluten, "glue") is a protein composite found in wheat and related grains, including barley and rye. Gluten gives viscoelasticity|elasticity to dough, helping it leavening agent|rise and keep its shape and often gives the final product a chewiness|chewy texture.

Gluten is the composite of two storage proteins, gliadin and a glutenin, and is conjoined with starch in the endosperm of various Triticeae glutens|grass-related cereal|grains. Worldwide, gluten is a source of protein, both in foods prepared directly from sources containing it, and as an food additive|additive to Low-protein diet|foods otherwise low in protein.

The fruit of most flowering plants have endosperms with stored protein to nourish Plant embryogenesis|embryonic plants during germination. True gluten is limited to certain members of the Poaceae|grass family. The stored proteins of maize and rice are sometimes called glutens, but their proteins differ from true gluten.
