Ptychopetalum is a genus of two species of flowering plants in the family Olacaceae (not to be confused with the Oleaceae or olive family), native to the Amazon rainforest.
The indigenous name for the genus is Muira Puama, "potency wood".
The species are shrubs or small trees growing to about 14 feet in height.
Its leaves are short-Petiole petioled, up to 3inches in length and 2inches in breadth light green on upper surface, dark brown on lower surface.
The inflorescences consist of short axillary racemes of 4 to 6 flowers each.
