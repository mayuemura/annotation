Spirulina is a cyanobacterium (blue-green algae) that can be consumed by humans and other animals.
There are two species, Arthrospira platensis and Arthrospira maxima.
Arthrospira is cultivated worldwide; used as a dietary supplement as well as a whole food; and is also available in tablet, flake and powder form.
It is also used as a fodder|feed supplement in the aquaculture, aquarium and poultry industries.
